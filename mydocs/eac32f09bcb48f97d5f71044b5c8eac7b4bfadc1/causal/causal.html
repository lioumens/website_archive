6.1 Propensity Scores

What are propensity scores? A propensity score is defined as the probability of a treatment assignment conditional on some background observed covariates.

This allows one to mimic some of the conditions of a randomized controlled trial. In a randomized control trial, one can be fairly certain that each group is balanced due to the law of large numbers.
